  • A real proof is a thing of beauty, but it takes quite a bit of work to appreciate this. If this course is successful, then at least once before the end of the semester, the beauty of a deductive proof will smack you hard right between the eyes. You will shed tears of joy, and you will be forever changed. (Unfortunately, this form of success is difficult to test, and does not guarantee a passing grade.)

    There is actually quite a bit more to logic than proof, however. Symbolic logic is the most precise form of notation ever developed, and has been absolutely fundamental to contemporary developments in in mathematics, linguistics, computer science and, yes, philosophy. Simply becoming comfortable with logical notation is an enormous benefit to anyone who would like to do advanced work in these fields. More generally, the course will also develop your ability to think carefully and precisely in an abstract way, which will be useful to you in all future studies.

    At the completion of this course, you will be able to explain key concepts such as consistency, contradiction, identity, tautology, and validity, use techniques such as truth tables and natural deduction to evaluate these concepts, formalize statements in natural language, and express and understand propositions in the languages of sentence and predicate logic.

  • What this means is that you will study at your own pace and you will decide when to take a particular test. After the first week of the semester it will become possible to take tests during any class meeting.

    Nevertheless, the class has a structured progression, so perhaps it's best describe it as semi-self-paced, for it will never be possible to take more than one test per class meeting. As a result, there will become a point where it is logically impossible to take enough tests to pass the class. Hence, it is extremely important for you to keep yourself on a strict test taking schedule. Moreover, you will not be permitted to take a test until you have passed the previous one at a designated level. This level will be high, corresponding to a strong B or better in almost all cases. The reason for this is that the material is progressive. If you do not master the material at a certain level, you will find the subsequent level impossible to understand.

  • If you are a self-motivated student who can do steady consistent work, you should succeed in this class without much issues. On the other hand, if you thrive under guidance, I will provide individual attention based on your style and needs. This course is not designed to have you do everything on your own, but to give the help and structure that suit each student's individual needs.

    At any time, feel free to speak to me about ways in which your learning could be better facilitated. The openness and freedom offered by the structure of this course allows the course to adapt to your learning style, and not the other way around.

  • Extensive homework assignments will be available for every module of the course. The problems will mostly taken from our texts, as will the solutions. You do not submit homework, but you will receive help with troublesome problems in class.

  • Your grade in this course will be calculated on the basis of 12 tests. These are all closed book in-class tests. In order to receive a B or above in this class you must additionally complete the number of participatory assignments required for each grade. See specific requirements for assignments below.

    There is no penalty for failing a test - your grade will not be affected and you are allowed to retake the test as many times as you like, assuming we have the class time for it. However, keep in mind that you are allowed to take one test per class period.

  • Prior to taking any given test you will be required to take and pass a corresponding quiz. These quizzes are will be multiple choices and you will have 15 min to finish the quiz. You may not take any test prior to passing the corresponding quiz. You can request to take a quiz at the beginning of each class session. Your grades on quizzes do not figure into your overall course grade.

    If you pass the quiz, you will be permitted to take the test in the same session, or any class afterward. If you fail the quiz, the quiz will be returned to you with the number of incorrect answers on it; however, it will not specify which of the answer(s) were incorrect. Nevertheless, you are free to consult your notes and/or collaborate with your classmates. I will also answer any question you may have, though I will not directly give you the answers.

    Normally, I would not permit the failed quiz to be retaken on the same day, for your time would be better spent on carefully studying the materials and working homework problems, instead of simply guessing over and over again until you stumble on the right answer. However, I may make an exception if I feel that you are sufficiently close to passing.

  • In order to get a B+ or above in this class, you are required to complete participatory assignments (PAs) in addition to taking the tests. For each grade from B+ or above, there is a corresponding number of projects required, as specified in the table above. For your PA to qualify for credit, it must be approved by me beforehand.

    For example, to get a B+ in this class, you need to pass 10 tests and complete one PA. To get an A you need to past all 12 tests, and complete three PAs, but you are free to take on a PA at any time, so long as it is approved by me.

    You are encouraged to work on these assignments in class, and ask me for clarification and/or guidance.

  • One way to receive a B+ or above in this class is to help other students learn by spending time in class tutoring. These opportunities will be arise when the need arises, and I will offer inform the students who have reached the appropriate level the opportunity. These tutoring sessions will occur during class time, but not during the test period (last 30 minutes of the class).

    • One option to fulfill your PA requirement is to give fifteen minutes presentation on a narrowly and strictly defined topic, approved by me. The easier topic would be to discuss an idea covered in the text, and a detailed walk-through of a problem that illustrates the concepts you intended to cover.

    It is possible to present on novel subjects, given my approval. For instance, you might give a presentation on how you used logic in your work for other courses, or you could present on the history of the development in logic. There is also a plethora of philosophical topics you could present.

    Another option is to submit your own logic problems, modeled after the ones in assignments, tests, and exams. You must provide solutions to these problems. Your work must be reasonably substantial - it should amount to roughly a page and a half of work.
